# Media Dock 3 demo design

## Identity

The demo extends Media Dock 3's accepted **Luminous Workshop** language: a calm, light-first local workspace where a Source enters the dock, becomes a Task, and leaves as a dependable Deliverable. It must feel like the product itself, not a separate neon launch campaign.

## Palette

- Canvas: `#f4f1eb`
- Canvas strong: `#ebe7df`
- Surface: `#fbfaf7`
- Ink: `#22262c`
- Ink soft: `#656b73`
- Ink faint: `#92979e`
- Sea-glass cyan: `#78cddb`
- Cyan strong: `#389eb2`
- Soft lilac: `#9f8bd0`
- Warm amber: `#e5bb69`
- Success: `#6eaf91`

## Typography

- Display and Latin product statements: `Montserrat`, weight 900.
- Chinese statements: `Noto Sans JP`, weight 700.
- Product metadata and version labels: `Space Mono`, weights 400 and 700.
- Headlines are 74–104 px; feed-readable support copy is at least 32 px.

## Geometry and depth

- Editorial split frames anchored to the canvas edges.
- Interface screenshots keep their real rounded product geometry inside a 28 px presentation frame.
- Corners use 20, 28, or 36 px radii.
- Depth is quiet and warm: soft graphite shadows, solid structural rules, no glass walls.

## Motion

- Restrained medium energy: decisive entrances, gentle camera drift, blur crossfades, and one vertical push into the final brand scene.
- Every scene enters; transitions perform the exits.
- Motion communicates source arrival, task formation, and successful delivery. No perpetual shimmer or decorative floating loops.

## Avoid

- No cyber control-room styling, neon-on-black, generic AI gradients, card walls, or literal tool logos.
- No Cookie values, names, domains, paths, account data, or support-log contents.
- No invented product screens and no fake terminal activity.
